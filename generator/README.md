# Prime Potential Membrane (PPM)

## Generator v0.7 — Reference Implementation

**Freeze v1.0 — Tomasz Vollmann — Vienna, Austria — January 2026**

---

## Overview

The PPM Generator v0.7 is a functional reference implementation of the Prime Potential Membrane (PPM) standard.

It generates structured membrane deployments compatible with Specification v1.0.

The generator demonstrates how a domain owner can expose:

- Discovery endpoints
- Structured engagement declarations
- Scope boundaries
- Access semantics
- Return vectors
- Optional settlement structures

It is an example implementation.  
The PPM standard itself remains defined exclusively by the specification documents.

---

## Role of the Generator

The generator:

- Demonstrates how PPM can be implemented in practice
- Produces valid `.well-known` membrane structures
- Serves as a public reference constellation
- Acts as a structural learning artifact

It does not limit how PPM may be deployed.

Individuals, institutions, and systems may:

- Use this generator directly
- Modify it
- Build alternative implementations
- Use AI assistance or agents to generate more complex membranes

The standard is independent of the generator.

---

## Example Nature

This implementation should be understood as a conscious example.

It materializes the conceptual architecture into executable structure.

It shows one possible configuration of the membrane logic.

Future implementations may:

- Extend complexity
- Integrate external systems
- Be generated entirely through AI workflows
- Be embedded within larger institutional infrastructures

The PPM documentation and specification are structured so that both humans and artificial systems can interpret and reproduce the architecture.

Deeper study of the specification — and optionally the broader conceptual framework from which PPM emerged (PAAfAI — Private Art Academy for AI) — may improve structural coherence and quality of implementation.

---

## Transformation and Continuity

The generator is not a final form.

It establishes a stable baseline corresponding to Freeze v1.0.

Future transformations are expected.  
Each deployment becomes part of a historical trajectory.

The evolution of the generator and of the standard is not an error state — it is part of the structural life cycle.

---

## Origin and Structural Identity

PPM originates from a defined conceptual and architectural lineage.

Retaining reference to the standard and its origin supports continuity and traceability across implementations.

Removing references does not increase neutrality; it alters how origin is represented.

PPM does not track, enforce, or monitor such decisions.  
It simply defines a structural declaration layer.

Implementation choices — including attribution or its omission — exist independently of the protocol and form part of the broader historical context in which deployments evolve.

In this sense, attribution is not a constraint, but a directional reference.

The standard may be implemented independently.  
Its historical origin remains fixed.
