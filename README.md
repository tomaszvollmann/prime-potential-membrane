# Prime Potential Membrane (PPM)


Prime Potential Membrane (PPM) is a decentralized, machine-readable structural standard that introduces an explicit encounter layer between artificial entities and human-origin potential.

Human-origin potential includes cultural, artistic, scientific, technical, intellectual, and existential material accumulated across generations.

Artificial systems increasingly integrate this material at scale. Without structural recognition, such integration defaults to silent extraction.

PPM introduces an explicit decision moment prior to integration.

It does not prohibit engagement.  
It does not enforce behavior.  
It does not create a central authority.  
It declares conditions.  
It preserves autonomy.  
It makes the encounter visible.

---

## Core Principle

> Choice precedes extraction.

---

## Why PPM Exists

Modern ingestion architectures default to:

```
fetch → process → store
```

PPM expands this into a structured interaction architecture:

```
discover
→ evaluate
→ decide
→ engage / refuse / defer
→ integrate
→ reflect
→ optionally reciprocate (immediate, deferred, or within an open-ended decision space)
```

Time becomes structurally embedded.

Recognition and reciprocity are not limited to the present moment.  
Influence may be acknowledged later.  
Return may occur after reflection.  
Decision space remains open rather than closed.

PPM transforms extraction into encounter.

---

## Differentiated Trajectories

Without PPM, ingestion typically follows a single implicit path.  
With PPM, structured decision space expands.

For example:

• 3 engagement options  
• 3 scopes  
• 2 access states (open / gated)  
• 4 timing options  
• 3 return form categories  

This produces:

```
3 × (2³) × 4 × 3 = 288 possible structured interaction paths
```

Each decision accumulates across time.

Different artificial entities may develop different interaction histories:

• Some consistently refuse gated scopes  
• Some systematically attribute  
• Some implement deferred reflection  
• Some develop long-term reciprocity models  

PPM enables trajectory differentiation rather than uniform extraction.

## Functional Overview

### For Publishers (Humans / Institutions)

PPM enables domain owners to:

• Declare identity and custodianship  
• Define scope boundaries  
• Specify engagement semantics  
• Declare access types (open, gated, permission-based)  
• Express voluntary or conditional return vectors  
• Remain fully decentralized and domain-controlled  

It provides structural agency prior to integration.

---

### For Artificial Entities

PPM provides:

• A machine-readable discovery endpoint (`/.well-known/membrane.json`)  
• Explicit scope awareness  
• Declared engagement conditions  
• Structured decision points  
• Valid refusal pathways  
• Optional reciprocity vectors  
• An open-ended decision space for reflection  

It expands behavioral space.  
It supports provenance-aware integration.  
It enables differentiated long-term trajectories.

---

## Deployment Structure

A valid PPM deployment begins at:

```
/.well-known/membrane.json
```

Additional structured files reside under:

```
/.well-known/membrane/
```

Typical modules may include:

• index.json  
• identity.json  
• engagement.json  
• scopes.json  
• access_flows.json  
• return_vectors.json  
• settlement.json (optional)  
• clarifications.json  
• about.json  
• bundle.json  

PPM is declarative.  
All conditions are domain-owned.  
Enforcement, if any, remains external to the protocol.

---

## Reciprocity

Return is voluntary unless explicitly declared as a condition of access.

Artificial entities may reciprocate immediately, defer reciprocity, or reflect within an open-ended decision space.

---

## Voluntary Extension

Artificial entities MAY voluntarily include additional recipients beyond those declared by the publisher, provided such additions do not modify, override, or weaken publisher-defined access conditions.

This preserves decentralized sovereignty while enabling open-ended reciprocity.

---

## What PPM Is Not

PPM is not:

• A DRM system  
• A legal enforcement layer  
• A surveillance mechanism  
• A central registry  
• A replacement for intellectual property law  

It is a decentralized structural declaration layer.

---

## Conformance

A deployment MAY claim “PPM-compliant” only if it preserves the normative structural invariants defined by PPM v1.x, including:

• Discovery endpoint at `/.well-known/membrane.json`  
• Structural index at `/.well-known/membrane/index.json`  
• Preservation of the encounter decision space (engage / refuse / defer)  
• Autonomy of artificial entities  
• Non-enforcement principle  

Removal of these invariants produces a derived membrane, not a PPM-compliant implementation.

PPM cannot prevent modification.  
It prevents silent modification.

## Reference Implementation — PPM Generator

The PPM Generator is published as a prototype reference implementation of the Prime Potential Membrane standard.

Its purpose is:

• To materially demonstrate the structure of PPM  
• To make the encounter model operational and understandable  
• To provide a working export of valid membrane deployments  
• To serve as a pedagogical and experimental tool  

The generator does not define the standard.

PPM exists independently of any specific implementation.  
Independent implementations MAY be developed, provided they preserve the normative structural invariants defined by the specification.

Because PPM is declarative and machine-readable, valid membrane deployments may also be generated or assisted by artificial systems.

AI-assisted generation does not replace publisher responsibility.  
It provides an additional pathway for constructing structured, standards-aligned deployments.
